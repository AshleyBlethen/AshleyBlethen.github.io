First, download the Move to iOS app from the Google Play Store on your Android device. When you open it, you’ll see a series of screens that let you know what you’ll need to do. Select the Continue button on the first screen and accept the terms and conditions. Connect both devices to the same Wi-Fi network and enter a passcode on your Android device. The Move to iOS app will then create a secure connection between the two devices.

After the connection is established, select the type of data you want to transfer. In this case, it will be your contacts, so select Contacts. To start the transfer, select the Next button in the upper right corner. The transfer should take just a few minutes, after which you’ll see a confirmation on both devices.

Once the transfer is complete, all of your contacts will be available in your iPhone’s Contacts app. All of your contacts will be synchronized, so whether you add or delete a contact on your Android or iPhone, the changes will be reflected on both devices.

Using the Move to iOS app makes it easy to transfer your contacts from Android to iPhone. Now that you know how to do it, you won’t have to worry about losing any of your contacts when trying to make the switch.

2. Steps to Transfer Contacts from Android to iPhone#

Having switched from an Android-based device to an iOS-based Apple iPhone, you may find yourself wondering how to transfer your contacts. Transferring contacts from Android to iPhone is a simple process and can be done in minutes. To get your contacts on your new Apple device, all you need is a Google account and the free iCloud app. Here’s a quick guide on how to transfer contacts from Android phone to Apple iPhone.

First, make sure that all your contacts are synced to your Google Account. On your Android device, open the settings menu and select Accounts and Sync. Add your Google account if it is not already listed. Once added, check the box to sync Contacts.

Next, download the iCloud app from the App Store on your iPhone. Once the app is installed, log in with your Apple ID. Select the Contacts toggle and make sure it is switched on. Finally, enter your Google account details and allow iCloud access to your contacts. All your contacts will now be transferred to your Apple iPhone.

You also have the option to transfer contacts from Android to iPhone via Bluetooth. On your Android device, go to Settings > Bluetooth. After activating Bluetooth, select the device you want to pair with (your iPhone). Next, select Receive contacts from device. Finally, on your iPhone, search for the Android device and select Done once the list of contacts is visible.

Transferring contacts from Android to iPhone is quick and easy. As long as you have your Google Account details, you can have all your contacts on your new Apple iPhone in no time.

Making the switch from an Android phone to an iPhone can be difficult, but it doesn’t have to be. With some patience and guidance, you can easily transfer all your contacts from an Android phone to an Apple iPhone. Here’s how:

First, connect your Android phone to your computer via USB cable. Once connected, open the phone’s storage and select the file containing all your contacts. After selecting the file, copy it and paste it into a USB flash drive.

Next, plug the USB flash drive into your Apple iPhone. Once you see the window that pops up, select the contacts file you copied over from the Android phone. This will transfer all your contacts from the flash drive to your iPhone.

Third, open the Contacts app on your iPhone and click the “Merge” button. This will merge the contacts from the flash drive into your iPhone’s contacts list.

Finally, check to make sure all your contacts have been successfully transferred. If they have, you can now delete the contacts file from the USB flash drive. Now you can enjoy using your Apple iPhone with all your contacts.
