1. How to Take a Screenshot via Assistive Touch#

Do you have an iPhone 6 with a broken Home button? Screenshots can be a helpful tool for documentations, conversations, and more. Thankfully, Apple has offered an option that allows you to still take screenshots with a broken Home button. Read on to learn how to take a screenshot via Assistive Touch.

Assistive Touch is a feature on your iPhone 6 that provides a virtual Home button which, among other features, allows you to take screenshots. To enable this feature, go to Settings, then General, Accessibility. Then, enable the Assistive Touch option.

Now that Assistive Touch is activated, you can start taking screenshots. A small Assistive Touch icon will appear on the screen. To take a screenshot, tap the Assistive Touch icon, then select the Device option from the list of options. Then, tap on the More option followed by the Screenshot option. Your screenshot will be saved automatically in your Photos App.

Finally, if you want to disable the Assistive Touch feature, all you have to do is return to the Assistive Touch settings and turn it off.

Assistive Touch is an extremely useful feature for anyone with an iPhone 6 that has a broken Home button. If you ever find yourself in this situation, trying out Assistive Touch is definitely recommended.

Now whenever you need to take a screenshot, you can simply open the Assistive Touch from the home screen and click on the ‘Screenshot’ icon. You’ll then be able to take a screenshot and access it from the ‘Photos’ gallery.

B. Taking a Screenshot with Assistive Touch#

A broken home button on an iPhone 6 can be frustrating. Luckily, you can still take a screenshot by using Assistive Touch. This feature makes it possible to access various functions without the need of a physical button. To take a screenshot on an iPhone 6 with a broken home button, follow these steps:

First, open the “Settings” app and select “General.” Scroll down and tap “Accessibility,” followed by “Assistive Touch.” Turn the Assistive Touch feature on by tapping the toggle switch.

Next, a gray circle with a white center will appear on your screen. Tap it and select “Device” and “More.” A menu will appear. Select “Screenshot” to take a picture of your screen.

You should now have a screenshot of your screen. You can view it in your photos app or share it with others.

Lastly, if you do not want the floating Assistive Touch circle to be on your screen, go back to Settings > General > Accessibility > Assistive Touch and toggle the feature off. This will hide the circle until you turn it back on.
