There is also the option to save the screenshot directly to your laptop’s hard drive. To do this, press the Windows + Print Screen button. The image will be automatically saved to your laptop’s Pictures folder.

In addition, you can also edit your screenshots before saving them. To edit the screenshot, open the image in an image editing application like Photoshop or GIMP. You can then alter the image in whatever way you like. Once you are happy with the result, you can save the image to your Pictures folder.

3. How to Take Screenshots with Snipping Tool#

Taking screenshots on Samsung laptops is an easy task with the built-in Snipping Tool. To begin, simply press the Windows key + R to launch the Run box. Once opened, type in “Snipping Tool” and press Enter. This will launch the Snipping Tool application. When the application opens, choose the type of Snip you would like to capture. There are 4 types to choose from: free-form, rectangular, window and full-screen snip. Then, use your mouse to draw on the screen the area you want to capture. To confirm your selection, press the “New” button. This will take a screenshot of your selection and open it in the Snipping Tool application. From here, you can edit, annotate or save the snip to your computer. To save the snip, click the Save Snip button in the top-left corner. To finish, you will be asked to provide a name and location for the file. Once done, click “Save” and the screenshot will be saved to your computer. With that, you have now successfully taken a screenshot with Snipping Tool.
