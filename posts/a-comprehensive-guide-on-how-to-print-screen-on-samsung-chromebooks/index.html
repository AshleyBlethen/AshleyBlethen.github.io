This will capture the entire screen and save it to your Chromebook’s Downloads folder. You can then access this image from the ‘Files’ app or drag it into any folder you wish.

Alternatively, if you want to take a screenshot of a specific window or region on your Chromebook, you can press the ‘Ctrl’, ‘Shift’, and ‘Window Switcher’ keys simultaneously. This will bring up a selection box which you can drag and resize to select the area of your screen that you wish to capture. When you’ve decided on the area that you want to capture, all you need to do is press the ‘Enter’ key and the selected area will be saved to your Downloads folder.

Another method you can use to take a screenshot on your Samsung Chromebook is with the Chrome OS Shortcut Keys. To capture your entire screen, press ‘Ctrl’+‘Shift’+‘Fn’+‘,’ (comma). You can also take a screenshot of a portion of your screen by pressing ‘Ctrl’+‘Shift’+‘Fn’+‘.’ (period). Both of these methods will save the screenshots to your Downloads folder.

Finally, if you want to take a screenshot of your entire screen with annotations or arrows, you can use the Screen Capture tool. This tool can be found in the Chrome OS Settings and can be used to quickly take a screenshot with annotations and arrows. Once you’ve taken the screenshot with the annotations, all you need to do is save it and it will be saved to your Downloads folder.

Taking screenshots on your Samsung Chromebook is easy and convenient with the various methods discussed in this article.
